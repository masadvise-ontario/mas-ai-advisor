# What a MAS engagement looks like

**Source value**: `engagement-description`
**Audience scope**: `mas_public`

Management Advisory Service (MAS) is a Canadian-charter charity that has supported the nonprofit sector since 1994. About one hundred senior business volunteers ("Volunteer Consultants" or VCs) donate their time to small nonprofits that can't afford management consulting. The areas MAS works in are strategic planning, fundraising, governance, communications, operations — and, increasingly, AI adoption.

## How an engagement starts

A nonprofit submits a project request at **[masadvise.org/contact-us](https://www.masadvise.org/contact-us/)**. MAS's Project Co-ordinator reviews the request, has an intake conversation to scope the work, and matches the project to two or three suitable VCs. The VCs and the nonprofit's executive director then meet to confirm fit, scope, and timing before the engagement formally begins.

## Cost model — pro bono with invited donation

MAS engagements are pro bono. The nonprofit pays a modest administrative fee (typically a few hundred dollars) to cover MAS's coordination overhead — not the volunteers' time. The volunteers donate their time.

If the engagement delivers real value, MAS asks for an **invited donation** at the end — proportional to what the work would have cost on the open market, and only what the nonprofit can afford. If the engagement didn't deliver value, there is no expectation. The framing is deliberate: MAS exists because the sector deserves access to senior advisory capacity it couldn't otherwise reach, and the donation model keeps that access open for the next nonprofit in line.

## Shape and timing

Most engagements run **four to ten weeks**, with a clearly scoped problem and a defined handoff. The shape is meeting-based: the VCs and the client meet every one or two weeks, work between meetings, and end with a deliverable the client can act on — a strategic plan, a fundraising approach, a governance review, a chatbot scoped to their needs, whatever the engagement was about.

Some engagements stay narrower and shorter (a single advisory conversation, a one-week sprint on a tight question). Some grow longer when the client asks for a follow-on phase. The starting frame is *short, scoped, and handed off* — not an ongoing retainer.

## What a MAS engagement is not

- **Not a managed service.** MAS doesn't run things for clients on an ongoing basis. The deliverable is something the client owns and operates.
- **Not a vendor pitch.** MAS isn't selling software, tools, or platforms. The volunteers' job is to advise, not to upsell.
- **Not a substitute for in-house capacity.** MAS works best alongside a client team that's ready to act on the advice — not as a replacement for one.

## How to start

Submit a project request at **[masadvise.org/contact-us](https://www.masadvise.org/contact-us/)**. The intake form asks for a brief description of the organization and the help you're looking for. MAS responds within a few business days to schedule the intake conversation.

If you're unsure whether your need fits, submit anyway and ask. The Project Co-ordinator will tell you honestly whether MAS is the right fit, and if it isn't, will usually be able to point you somewhere that is.
